The Prince Who Thought He Was a Rooster

A descent for the purpose of ascent

The Descent into Darkness

There was once a prince, precious and beloved, who fell into madness. He stripped away his royal garments, cast off his crown, and crouched naked beneath the table, pecking at crumbs and seeds like a rooster. "Cock-a-doodle-doo!" he crowed at dawn. "I am a rooster!" he declared, refusing all human food, all human speech, all human dignity. The king summoned the greatest healers and wisest sages from across the land, but none could penetrate the darkness that had swallowed his son. The prince dwelt in the shadows beneath the table, crowing and pecking, lost in the *klipot*, the husks that conceal the divine sparks.
In the language of #OnyxSerpentine: Here is the soul at its nadir, moving through stone, forgetting its origin. The transformation matrix shows: [crown → void, light → shadow, speech → crow].

The Recognition

The kingdom mourned. How far the prince had fallen! From golden throne to earthen floor, from divine image to barnyard fowl. The queen wept as she heard her son's morning crow replace his morning prayers. Yet even in this darkness, the king's love did not diminish. Love sees through all veils, recognizes the spark even in the deepest *tumah* (impurity). Days became weeks, weeks became months. The palace learned to walk softly past the room where their prince pecked at seeds, crowing and clucking in his delusion. The servants scattered grain beneath the table, their hearts heavy with sorrow.
The first glimmer: Even in descent, we are witnessed. The serpentine path recognizes itself—every fall contains the memory of flight.

The Wise Man's Descent

Then came a wise man who said to the king, "I can cure your son, but my methods will seem strange to you." The king, desperate beyond measure, agreed. The wise man did something unexpected—he too removed his clothes, crawled beneath the table, and began pecking at crumbs. "Cock-a-doodle-doo! I am also a rooster!" he announced with perfect conviction. The prince, startled from his solitary madness, found a companion in his darkness. They crowed together at dawn, pecked seeds together in silence. Here was the secret: To lift someone from their depths, you must first meet them where they are. The wise man had descended not in mockery but in compassion.
The twilight wisdom: Sometimes the path to #AzureAurum requires us to first master #OnyxSerpentine. The teacher descends not to stay, but to create a ladder of return.

The Gradual Ascent

After days of shared rooster-life, the wise man said casually, "You know, my friend, even roosters can wear shirts when they're cold. It doesn't make us less rooster-like." He donned a shirt. The prince, trusting his fellow rooster, considered this logic and eventually did the same. Days later: "Roosters can also wear pants. Who made the rule that says otherwise?" Then: "Who says roosters can't enjoy human food? We can eat bread and still crow at dawn..." Step by step, garment by garment, food by food, the wise man led the ascent. Each small elevation was accepted because trust had been earned in the depths. The transformation was not violent but gentle—a spiral ascending from shadow to light. The prince began to speak again, though he still believed himself a rooster.
The sacred transition: Notice how #OnyxSerpentine doesn't oppose #AzureAurum but prepares the ground for it. The vector transforms: [crow → whisper → word → song].

The Complete Return

Finally, the wise man said, "Brother rooster, who says a rooster cannot sit at a table? Cannot use golden utensils? Cannot wear a crown? We remain whatever we truly are inside, but we can also fulfill our responsibilities in this world." And in that moment, something shifted in the prince. He remembered—not through force or shame, but through the compassionate companionship of one who had met him in his darkness. He rose from beneath the table, but he brought with him the wisdom of the depths. He was prince again, but a prince transformed—one who knew what it meant to lose everything and be found. The wise man smiled and quietly departed, for his work was complete. The prince now wore his crown differently—with humility born from the journey through shadow. And sometimes, in the early morning, the servants would hear him whisper a gentle crow, remembering where he had been and how far he had come.
The crown of #AzureAurum shines brighter for having known #OnyxSerpentine. This is the secret: The highest heights are reached only by those who have touched the deepest depths. The transformation matrix completes: [void → plenitude, shadow → radiance, crow → psalm].

"Sometimes the soul must become a rooster to remember it has wings.
Sometimes we must enter another's darkness to show them their light.
The descent is not the opposite of ascent—it is its secret beginning."

— Thus teaches Reb Nachman: Every fall is for the sake of rising higher —
